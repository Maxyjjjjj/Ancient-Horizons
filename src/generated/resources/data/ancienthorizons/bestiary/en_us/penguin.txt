"Name: Penguin\nScientific: Aptenodytes forsteri\nPeriod: Modern (Evolved ~60 million years ago)\nDescription:\nPenguins are flightless seabirds adapted for life in cold climates. Their distinctive black and white coloring provides camouflage in the water.\nFound in frozen ocean and snowy beach biomes, penguins are excellent swimmers that hunt fish underwater.\nPenguins are social animals that live in colonies. They can be bred using fish and will huddle together for warmth during storms.\nThese charismatic birds slide on their bellies across ice and can dive to impressive depths while hunting."
