"Name: Golden Eagle\nScientific: Aquila chrysaetos\nPeriod: Modern\nDescription:\nThe golden eagle is one of the largest birds of prey in the world, known for its powerful build and impressive hunting skills.\nFound in mountain biomes, eagles soar on thermal currents searching for prey below. They have exceptional eyesight and can spot prey from great heights.\nEagles are protected species in many regions. Killing them may result in negative effects. They hunt small animals and will occasionally attack livestock.\nThese magnificent raptors mate for life and build massive nests called eyries on cliff faces and tall trees."
